10. Use caution on public Wi-Fi networks

Public Wi-Fi networks usually do not encrypt traffic. This means that anyone can keep track of what you send and receive by connecting to the same access point. Try not to send confidential information via social networks: usernames, passwords, credit card details, etc. It is best to use a VPN to encrypt data transfer and protect it from prying eyes.

  • Check the permissions you give to mobile apps. Here's how to do it in Android, and here's how to do it in iOSIf possible, do not use public Wi-Fi networks.
  • If you can't do without public Wi-Fi, use a secure VPN connection.
